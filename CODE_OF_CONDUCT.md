# Code of Conduct

This project follows the [Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/). The full text is at the link above; the summary below is what it means in practice for Vulnex.

## In short

- Be respectful in issues, pull requests, reviews, and any other project space. Disagree with ideas, not with people.
- No harassment, personal attacks, slurs, doxxing, sexualized content, or sustained disruption — in public or in private channels related to the project.
- Assume good faith. If a comment reads as hostile, ask for clarification before escalating.
- Keep discussion on-topic. Vulnex is a security-tooling project; off-topic political or ideological arguments belong elsewhere.

## Scope

This applies to every project space — the GitHub repo (issues, PRs, discussions, code review comments), any associated chat or email threads, and any public space where someone is representing the project (talks, social posts, etc.).

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that breaks this code, report it privately to **jawadsalem12345@gmail.com** with subject line `CONDUCT: <short description>`. Do not open a public issue.

I'll acknowledge the report in a reasonable time, keep it confidential, and respond proportionally — that may mean a private warning, asking someone to edit a comment, a temporary interaction limit, or a permanent ban from the project, depending on severity and pattern.

Reports made in good faith will not lead to retaliation. Reports made in bad faith (to harass or silence someone) will themselves be treated as a code-of-conduct violation.

## Enforcement guidelines

For the four-tier enforcement ladder (correction → warning → temporary ban → permanent ban) and the criteria for each, see the [Contributor Covenant 2.1 enforcement guidelines](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines). I follow those as written.
